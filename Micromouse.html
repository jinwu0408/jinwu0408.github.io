Micromouse

What is Micromouse?

“Micromouse is an event where small robot mice solve a 16x16 maze. The mice are completely autonomous robots that must find their way from a predetermined starting position to the central area of the maze unaided. The mouse will need to keep track of where it is, discover walls as it explores, map out the maze and detect when it has reached the goal. Once the optimal route has been found, the mouse will run that route in the shortest possible time.”

Hardware

Schematic:

We have decided to use STM32F446 as our Micro Controller. When designing the schematic, we constantly refer to the datasheet to see which pin is GPIO, clock, etc. Luckily, our mentor provided us the schematic symbol with footprint, which saved us some time of making everything.

For powering up the mouse, we need 5V and 3V3. The JST-PH connector on the left has the battery plugs into. Then, it goes to a switch on turing on or off the mouse. Having capacitors to prevent voltage spike and 5V and 3V3 regulator to step down V-battery. On the right, there a voltmeter to see if the batter is in a good condition.

The motors that we are using are DC step motors. When we choosing motors, we are down to Faulhabor motors or step gear motors. Faulhabor motors are more accurate and it comes with an encoder. However, the cost are too high. So that we decided to use gear motors with H-Bridge(TC4427) and encoders(ZXMHC3) to drive

On the left, it is the communication pins that we need to connect with the neuclo board to programm the mouse. On the right, it is a bluetooth divice that we initially planed to use for debug purposes. However, due to the time constraint, we decide to move on without the bluetooth.

IR sensor has two parts. On the left, it is the emmiter, which emmit IR signal. Then the receiver, which is on the right, would receive the signal and by calculating the time difference, we will able to get the distance that the signal has traveled.

This is a simple LED and Button schematic. With 4 LED be programmable with the micro controller and 1 LED direcly to the power to indicate the ON/OFF state of the mouse. The two button would be programmable too, with one for reset and one for future reference.

PCB Design:

It was my first time doing PCB. Even with the help from mentor, I still had some difficulties. For example, I was planning on have 4 layers, so that the wiring would be much simple with gound and power being a single layer. However, the cost of 4 layers board is 7 times more expensive than two layers, which is the reason of why we decided to stay with 2 layers.

This is the board that I done with my teammates. We realized that there is some mistakes made when we testing it. First, the power wires on the bottom is too thin, which couldn't conduct the current. We didn't found that mistake because it was not labeled correctly. Second, the Tof sensor layout on the top right is fliped, which means we need to put two sensors above the board and two sensors under the board.
